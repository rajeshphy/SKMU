<div style="text-align: center; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; margin-bottom: 20px;">
  <div style="font-size: 1.9rem; font-weight: 700; color: #5a2a27;">ABSTRACT</div>
</div>

<div style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; line-height: 1.9; text-align: justify;">
  This dissertation explores the role of symmetry in modern physics, focusing on its mathematical formulation and physical implications. The study begins with an introduction to continuous symmetries and Lie algebraic structures, which form the foundation for understanding conservation laws and invariant principles. Special emphasis is placed on generators of symmetry transformations such as translations and rotations.

  <br><br>

  The framework is extended to quantum mechanics, where operators and commutation relations encode symmetry properties within Hilbert space. The dissertation further investigates supersymmetry (SUSY), introducing superalgebra structures that unify bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom.

  <br><br>

  Applications of symmetry principles are discussed in various physical contexts, highlighting their importance in simplifying complex systems and revealing underlying conservation laws.
</div>

</div>

<div style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; line-height: 1.8;">
  <div style="font-size: 1.35rem; font-weight: 700; color: #1f3c88;">2.1 Lie Algebra</div>

  <br>

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">2.1.1 Definition</div>
  <div style="margin-top: 8px; text-align: justify;">A Lie algebra is a vector space with a commutator satisfying algebraic properties.</div>

  <br>

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">2.1.2 Generators</div>
  <div style="margin-top: 8px; text-align: justify;">Generators define infinitesimal symmetry transformations.</div>

  <br>

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">2.1.3 Generator of Translation</div>

  $$
  \hat{P} = -i\hbar \frac{d}{dx}
  $$

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">2.1.4 Rotation Generator</div>

  $$
  \hat{L} = \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{p}
  $$

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">2.1.5 Angular Momentum Operators</div>

  $$
  [L_i, L_j] = i\hbar \epsilon_{ijk} L_k
  $$
</div>

<div style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; line-height: 1.8;">
  <div style="font-size: 1.3rem; font-weight: 700; color: #1f3c88;">4.1 Superalgebra</div>

  <br>

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">4.1.1 Definition</div>
  <div style="margin-top: 8px; text-align: justify;">Supersymmetry relates bosons and fermions.</div>

  <br>

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">4.1.2 Lie Superalgebra Structure</div>
  <div style="margin-top: 8px; text-align: justify;">Includes even and odd generators.</div>

  <br>

  <div style="font-size: 1.15rem; font-weight: 700; color: #333;">4.1.3 Generators</div>
  <ul style="margin-top: 8px; line-height: 1.8;">
    <li>Hamiltonian (even)</li>
    <li>Supercharges (odd)</li>
  </ul>

  $$
  H = \{Q, Q^\dagger\}
  $$

  $$
  Q^2 = (Q^\dagger)^2 = 0
  $$

  $$
  \{Q, Q^\dagger\} = H
  $$
</div>
