# ABSTRACT

This dissertation explores the role of symmetry in modern physics, focusing on its mathematical formulation and physical implications. The study begins with an introduction to continuous symmetries and Lie algebraic structures, which form the foundation for understanding conservation laws and invariant principles. Special emphasis is placed on generators of symmetry transformations such as translations and rotations.

The framework is extended to quantum mechanics, where operators and commutation relations encode symmetry properties within Hilbert space. The dissertation further investigates supersymmetry (SUSY), introducing superalgebra structures that unify bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom.

Applications of symmetry principles are discussed in various physical contexts, highlighting their importance in simplifying complex systems and revealing underlying conservation laws.

# Chapter 4: Supersymmetry (SUSY)

## 4.1 Superalgebra

### 4.1.1 Definition  
Supersymmetry relates bosons and fermions.

### 4.1.2 Lie Superalgebra Structure  
Includes even and odd generators.

### 4.1.3 Generators  
- Hamiltonian (even)  
- Supercharges (odd)  

$$
H = \{Q, Q^\dagger\}
$$

$$
Q^2 = (Q^\dagger)^2 = 0
$$

$$
\{Q, Q^\dagger\} = H
$$
