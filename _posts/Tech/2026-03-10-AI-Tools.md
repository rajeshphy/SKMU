**Teachable Machine**  
Usage: A Google tool to train simple machine learning models (image, pose, sound) without coding. Users can upload examples like cat, dog, panda and the system learns to classify them.  
Comparative Analysis: Easier for beginners compared to **TensorFlow** or **PyTorch**, which require programming knowledge.

**Open Knowledge Maps**  
Usage: Visual discovery tool for academic literature that organizes research papers into knowledge clusters.  
Comparative Analysis: Unlike **Google Scholar**, it provides a visual map of research topics instead of a simple list of papers.

**Semantic Scholar**  
Usage: AI-powered academic search engine that helps researchers discover influential papers and citations.  
Comparative Analysis: More intelligent filtering and citation insights compared to **Google Scholar**.

**AI2 Paper Finder (Allen AI)**  
Usage: Tool from Allen Institute that helps locate relevant research papers using AI-based semantic understanding.  
Comparative Analysis: More research-focused and context aware than traditional keyword search engines like **Google Scholar**.

**Citation Gecko**  
Usage: Helps researchers discover related academic papers by exploring citation networks.  
Comparative Analysis: Unlike **Mendeley** or **Zotero**, it focuses specifically on citation relationship discovery.

**ChatPDF**  
Usage: Allows users to upload a PDF and interact with it through chat to ask questions, summaries, or explanations.  
Comparative Analysis: Similar to **AskYourPDF**, but generally simpler and faster for direct document interaction.

**AnswerThis**  
Usage: AI research assistant that extracts answers from multiple academic sources and papers.  
Comparative Analysis: More focused on research questions compared to general AI tools like **ChatGPT**.

**Storm Genie**  
Usage: Idea generation and brainstorming tool that helps create structured concepts and solutions.  
Comparative Analysis: Comparable to **Notion AI**, but designed more specifically for ideation workflows.

**Napkin AI**  
Usage: Converts rough ideas or notes into structured diagrams and visual explanations.  
Comparative Analysis: Similar to **Miro AI**, but more focused on transforming textual ideas into visuals.

**MyBib AI**  
Usage: Automatically generates citations and bibliographies for academic writing.  
Comparative Analysis: Easier and faster compared to manual citation tools like **Zotero** or **EndNote**.

**EditGPT**  
Usage: Edits, improves, and revises written content while keeping track of changes.  
Comparative Analysis: Works like an AI editor similar to **Grammarly**, but with stronger rewriting capabilities.

**Mentimeter**  
Usage: Interactive presentation tool for polls, quizzes, and real-time audience engagement.  
Comparative Analysis: More interactive for live classes compared to **Google Slides**.

**Bricks AI**  
Usage: AI tool for generating structured content and visual data presentations.  
Comparative Analysis: Comparable to **Notion AI**, but more focused on structured productivity workflows.

**10MinuteMail**  
Usage: Temporary email service used for quick registrations without revealing personal email.  
Comparative Analysis: Similar to **Temp Mail**, both provide disposable email addresses.

**Claude AI**  
Usage: Large language model useful for writing, analysis, and research assistance.  
Comparative Analysis: Comparable to **ChatGPT** but often stronger in long document reasoning.

**Humanize AI Pro**  
Usage: Converts AI-generated text into more natural and human-like writing.  
Comparative Analysis: Similar to **QuillBot**, but specifically designed for AI-text humanization.

**Gemini**  
Usage: Google’s multimodal AI assistant capable of text, image, and reasoning tasks.  
Comparative Analysis: Direct competitor to **ChatGPT**, integrated deeply with Google ecosystem.
